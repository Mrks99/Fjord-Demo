What are Hyperliquid Vaults?

The engine behind Fjord, explained in plain language.

A managed trading pool

A vault is a shared trading account on Hyperliquid, a major on-chain derivatives exchange. It is run by a vault leader — a professional trader or an algorithm. When you invest, your money trades alongside everyone else's, following the leader's strategy. Profits and losses are shared in proportion to your stake.

Why investors find them attractive

Transparency: every trade and the vault's full track record are recorded on a public blockchain — nothing is self-reported. Aligned incentives: leaders must keep their own capital invested (at least 5% of the vault) and earn a share of profits only when investors profit. Access: strategies normally reserved for professional traders, from a low minimum, with the freedom to withdraw.

What to keep in mind

Vaults trade leveraged derivatives — strategies can and do lose money, and a high past APR is no promise of future returns. Deposits are briefly locked after investing, and vault investments carry no deposit guarantee. A sensible approach: invest only money you can afford to lose, and judge a vault by its full history and drawdowns, not its best month.
